Bio

I'm a security researcher and writer learning in public. My focus sits at the intersection of offensive security and knowledge sharing — understanding how attacks work at a deep technical level, then writing about it clearly enough that others can learn too.

My current areas of focus are malware analysis, reverse engineering, and threat modelling. I document everything as I go — the wins, the dead ends, and the moments where things finally click. This blog is that documentation.

When I'm not writing, I'm in the lab — solving CTF challenges, building detection rules, and working through security books and courses. I believe the best defenders are the ones who think like attackers.
